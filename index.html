PUBLIC LAW 93-205
the disappearing endangered species act
Since it’s beginnings in 1973, the Endangered Species Act (ESA) has helped put 99% of the species ever under it’s protection on the road to recovery. Today, the ESA affords protections to 1,600 species of plants and animals and is the strongest law protecting biodiversity passed by any nation to date.
Despite it’s long-standing record of success, the future of the ESA is threatened by a changing environment and a lack of support in the government.
